This Chart Shows Who Marries CEOs, Doctors, Chefs and Janitors
By John Curran
June 20, 2016

When it comes to falling in love, it’s not just fate that brings people together—sometimes it’s their jobs. We scanned data from the National Bureau of Statistics' 2014 Census of the Maldives —which covers all 78,000 households—to find out how people are pairing up. Some of the matches seemed practical (the most common marriage is between grade-school teachers), and others had us questioning Cupid’s aim (why do female dancers have a thing for male welders?). High-earning women (doctors, lawyers) tend to pair up with their economic equals, while middle- and lower-tier women often marry up. In other words, female CEOs tend to marry other CEOs; male CEOs are OK marrying their secretaries.

METHODOLOGY: For any selected occupation, the chart highlights the five most common occupation/relationship matchups. Same-sex occupation/relationship matchups weren’t common enough to reach the top five in any occupation. So the chart also highlights the top male-male and female-female job matchups for each occupation.
